# Metformin

**Mortality Impact**: Extends life expectancy by ~3 years in diabetic patients; all-cause mortality -7% compared to non-diabetic medication users
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Extensive RCTs + epidemiological studies)
**Actionability**: Low (Prescription drug, requires medical supervision)

## Conclusion
Metformin is currently one of the "potential anti-aging drugs" with the strongest evidence. The TAME trial results will determine whether it becomes the first drug approved for an anti-aging indication. Use in non-diabetic populations remains controversial.

## Evidence
- UK Prospective Diabetes Study (UKPDS): Overweight type 2 diabetes patients treated with metformin had -36% all-cause mortality and -39% myocardial infarction
- Observational data: Diabetic patients taking metformin have longer life expectancy than non-diabetic control populations
- TAME trial (Targeting Aging with Metformin): The first large RCT targeting "aging delay," currently underway
- Anti-cancer potential: Multiple studies suggest metformin reduces risk of various cancers (lung cancer -20%, breast cancer -30%, etc.)
- Mechanisms: Activates AMPK pathway, inhibits mTOR, reduces inflammation, improves insulin sensitivity
- Risks: Lactic acidosis (extremely rare), vitamin B12 deficiency, gastrointestinal discomfort
- Controversy: Insufficient safety and efficacy data for long-term use in non-diabetic populations

## Do
1. Non-diabetic individuals should not self-prescribe—wait for TAME trial results
2. For diabetic patients: Metformin is a first-line medication; use as prescribed
3. Follow TAME trial progress (results expected 2026–2027)
4. If interested in anti-aging, prioritize exercise, diet, and sleep—these have stronger evidence
