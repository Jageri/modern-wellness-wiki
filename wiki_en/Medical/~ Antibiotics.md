# Antibiotics

**Mortality Impact**: Antibiotic misuse leads to resistant bacteria, indirectly increasing mortality risk
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Antibiotic resistance is a global public health crisis)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Use only for bacterial infections, complete the full course, follow medical advice. Don't take antibiotics for a cold.

## Evidence
- WHO: Antibiotic resistance is one of the top 10 global public health threats
- ~1.27 million people die from antibiotic-resistant infections annually
- China is a major antibiotic consumer, with per capita usage 10× that of the US
- Antibiotic misuse disrupts gut microbiota and increases allergy and autoimmune risks

## Do
1. Use antibiotics only for bacterial infections (as determined by a doctor)
2. Complete the full prescribed course; don't stop early
3. Take medications as prescribed; don't purchase on your own

## Don't
1. Don't take antibiotics for a cold—most colds are viral; antibiotics are ineffective
2. Don't purchase antibiotics on your own
3. Don't use antibiotics to "prevent" infection—no benefit, only harm

## Medical Red Flags
- No improvement after 3 days of medication
- Severe allergic reaction (rash, difficulty breathing)
- Severe diarrhea after medication (possible Clostridioides difficile infection)
