# Vaccination

**Mortality Impact**: Significantly reduces severe illness and mortality from specific infectious diseases
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Vaccines are among humanity's most successful public health interventions)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Vaccinate on schedule. Get the flu vaccine annually; get the shingles vaccine at 50+.

## Evidence
- Vaccines save ~3.5–5 million lives per year (WHO latest estimate) [1]
- Flu vaccine reduces hospitalization rates in the elderly by ~40%
- Shingles vaccine (Shingrix) reduces incidence risk by ~90% (age 70+, NEJM 2016) [2]
- HPV vaccine can prevent ~90% of cervical cancers
- **Note**: Large RCT and global surveillance data

## Do
1. Follow the national immunization schedule
2. Flu vaccine annually (especially for elderly and those with chronic conditions)
3. Shingles vaccine for those aged 50+
4. HPV vaccine for ages 9–45 (the earlier the better)

## Don't
1. Don't skip vaccination because "you can still get infected"—it reduces severe illness rates
2. Don't ignore mild vaccine side effects—normal immune response
3. Don't miss the optimal vaccination window

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent high fever >48 hours after vaccination
- Severe allergic reaction (difficulty breathing, widespread rash)
