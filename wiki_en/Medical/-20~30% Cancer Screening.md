# Cancer Screening

**Mortality Impact**: Early detection can significantly reduce cancer mortality
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Robust screening evidence for specific cancers)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Lung cancer (low-dose CT for high-risk groups), colonoscopy (starting at age 45), mammography, cervical HPV+TCT. Early detection, early treatment.

## Evidence
- Low-dose CT screening reduces lung cancer mortality by ~20% (NLST trial)
- Colonoscopy screening reduces colorectal cancer mortality by ~68%
- HPV+TCT screening reduces cervical cancer mortality by ~80%
- Early-stage breast cancer has a 5-year survival rate >95%

## Do
1. Lung cancer: Age 50+, 20 pack-year smoking history—annual low-dose CT
2. Colorectal cancer: Colonoscopy every 10 years starting at age 45
3. Breast cancer: Mammography every 1–2 years starting at age 40–50
4. Cervical cancer: HPV+TCT starting at age 21, every 3–5 years

## Don't
1. Don't panic excessively over elevated tumor markers—false positives are common
2. Don't skip screening—early detection is key to reducing cancer mortality
3. Don't do "comprehensive tumor marker" screening—high false positive rates, unclear benefits

## Medical Red Flags
- Unexplained weight loss, persistent cough, bloody stool, abnormal bleeding
- Abnormal findings requiring follow-up on health checkups
