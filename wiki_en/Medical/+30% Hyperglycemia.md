# Hyperglycemia / Diabetes

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +30% (type 2 diabetes)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Causal relationship established)
**Actionability**: High (controllable and preventable)

## Conclusion
Control carbohydrates + regular exercise + monitor blood glucose + take medications as prescribed. Prediabetes can be reversed.

## Evidence
- Type 2 diabetes patients have ~30% higher all-cause mortality than non-diabetic populations
- Diabetes is a leading cause of cardiovascular disease, kidney failure, blindness, and amputation
- Prediabetes (fasting glucose 5.6–6.9 mmol/L) can be reversed in ~58% of cases through lifestyle intervention
- Each 1% reduction in HbA1c reduces microvascular complication risk by ~37%

## Do
1. Control refined carbohydrates; prioritize whole grains
2. Regular exercise (improves insulin sensitivity)
3. Monitor blood glucose and HbA1c regularly
4. Take medications as prescribed; do not stop on your own

## Don't
1. Don't believe "diabetes can be cured"—currently it can only be controlled, not cured
2. Don't stop insulin on your own—may lead to diabetic ketoacidosis
3. Don't focus only on blood glucose—blood pressure and lipids are equally important

## Medical Red Flags
- Blood glucose persistently >16.7 mmol/L
- Symptoms of ketoacidosis (nausea, vomiting, fruity breath odor)
- Non-healing foot ulcers
