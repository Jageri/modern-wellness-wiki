# Probiotics

**Mortality Impact**: Insufficient evidence; no clear mortality benefit currently
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐ (Highly strain-specific; high research heterogeneity)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Prioritize obtaining probiotics from fermented foods (yogurt, kimchi, miso). Probiotic supplements are not a panacea.

## Evidence
- The health benefits of probiotics are highly dependent on specific strains
- Most commercial probiotic products lack clinical evidence for specific indications
- The association between gut microbiota and immune, metabolic, and mental health is under active investigation
- No large RCT has demonstrated that probiotic supplements reduce all-cause mortality

## Do
1. Eat fermented foods daily (yogurt, kimchi, miso, kombucha)
2. Dietary fiber feeds beneficial gut bacteria (more effective than direct supplementation)
3. If taking probiotics, choose specific strains with clinical evidence

## Don't
1. Don't expect probiotics to "cure everything"—strain specificity is high
2. Don't buy products without strain identification numbers—effects cannot be traced
3. Don't use probiotics as a substitute for proper medical treatment

## Medical Red Flags
- Chronic diarrhea, bloody stool
- Immunocompromised individuals should not self-supplement
