# Hypertension (Uncontrolled)

**Mortality Impact**: Primary risk factor for cerebrovascular events (stroke, myocardial infarction)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Causal relationship established)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Salt restriction + exercise + weight loss + medications as prescribed. Don't stop medication when blood pressure normalizes.

## Evidence
- ~1/3 of adults globally have hypertension
- Each 10 mmHg reduction in systolic blood pressure reduces major cardiovascular event risk by ~20%
- China has one of the highest stroke incidence rates globally; hypertension is the primary cause
- ~50% of cardiovascular deaths are attributable to hypertension

## Do
1. <5g salt daily, regular exercise, weight management
2. Home blood pressure monitoring (morning and evening, once each)
3. Take medications as prescribed; do not adjust on your own
4. DASH diet (high potassium, high calcium, high magnesium, low sodium)

## Don't
1. Don't stop medication when blood pressure normalizes—it will rebound
2. Don't rely solely on dietary therapy—moderate-to-severe hypertension requires medication
3. Don't ignore "asymptomatic" hypertension—no symptoms ≠ no damage

## Medical Red Flags
- Blood pressure >180/120 mmHg
- Headache, vomiting, blurred vision
- Sudden blood pressure elevation with chest pain
