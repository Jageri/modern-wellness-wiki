# COVID-19 / Infectious Diseases

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality significantly elevated in the year following COVID-19 infection (excess mortality 10–30%)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Extensive epidemiological data)
**Actionability**: Medium

## Conclusion
Infectious disease pandemics are not "a thing of the past." COVID-19 exposed population vulnerabilities in metabolic health and immune function—factors that also influence outcomes from other infectious diseases.

## Evidence
- COVID-19 excess mortality: An estimated 18.2 million excess deaths globally in 2020-2021; all-cause mortality in the year following infection is significantly higher than in control populations, including non-respiratory causes [1]
- COVID-19 significantly increases cardiovascular risk in the year after infection (stroke, arrhythmia, heart failure, etc.) [2]
- Long COVID: Persistent fatigue, brain fog, heart palpitations, and other symptoms affect long-term quality of life and work capacity
- Key risk factors: Obesity, diabetes, hypertension, immunocompromise—highly overlapping with metabolic health
- Influenza: ~290,000–650,000 respiratory deaths globally per year [3]
- Vaccine efficacy: COVID-19 vaccines reduce severe illness and death risk by ~80–92% [4]
- **Note**: Vaccine effectiveness decreases over time and with viral mutations

## Do
1. Get vaccinated on schedule (COVID-19 boosters, flu vaccine, pneumococcal vaccine)
2. Improving metabolic health is itself the best "pandemic preparation"
3. Ensure good ventilation in public spaces; wash hands frequently
4. Don't ignore "minor illnesses"—repeated infections cause cumulative immune system damage
5. Elderly and those with chronic conditions: get an annual flu vaccine
