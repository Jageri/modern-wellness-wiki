# High Cholesterol

**Mortality Impact**: Primary modifiable risk factor for cardiovascular events
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Robust statin RCT evidence)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
LDL-C is the key metric. Exercise + dietary control + statins when necessary. Don't fear statins.

## Evidence
- Each 1 mmol/L reduction in LDL-C reduces major vascular event risk by ~22%
- Statins are the most evidence-supported lipid-lowering medications
- ~40% of Chinese adults have dyslipidemia, with low awareness and control rates
- LDL-C >4.9 mmol/L is associated with significantly elevated cardiovascular risk

## Do
1. Regular lipid panel testing (total cholesterol, LDL-C, HDL-C, triglycerides)
2. Exercise + dietary control (reduce saturated and trans fats)
3. When LDL-C is not at target, take medications as prescribed (statins are first-line)
4. For those with cardiovascular disease history: LDL-C target <1.8 mmol/L

## Don't
1. Don't focus only on total cholesterol—LDL-C is the key metric
2. Don't fear statins—benefits far outweigh risks (muscle soreness incidence <5%)
3. Don't assume "thin people can't have high cholesterol"—genetic factors also matter

## Medical Red Flags
- LDL-C >4.9 mmol/L
- Existing cardiovascular disease
- Severe muscle soreness after starting statins
