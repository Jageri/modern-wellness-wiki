# Sedentary Behavior

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +20–40% (sitting >6 hours/day, varies by sex)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistent across numerous prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
The increased mortality risk from prolonged sitting can be eliminated through sufficient physical activity. Get up and move for 5 minutes every 60 minutes. Approximately 60–75 minutes of moderate-intensity activity per day essentially offsets the mortality risk of prolonged sitting, though the harms of TV/screen-based sitting are harder to fully eliminate.

## Evidence
- Ekelund 2016 Lancet meta-analysis (16 studies, 1,005,791 individuals): Those sitting >8 hours/day in the lowest activity quartile had HR 1.59 (95% CI 1.52–1.66) for all-cause mortality; however, 60–75 min/day of moderate-intensity activity eliminated this risk (highest activity quartile HR 1.04, 95% CI 0.99–1.10, not statistically significant) [1]
- Patel 2010 American Cancer Society prospective cohort (127,554 US adults): Women sitting >6 vs <3 hours/day had approximately 40% higher mortality; men had approximately 20% higher mortality—notable sex difference [2]
- Note: The harm of TV/screen-viewing time was not fully eliminated even in the highest activity quartile (HR 1.13, 95% CI 1.04–1.23), suggesting screen-based sitting differs from total sitting time [1]
- Primary mechanisms: Reduced insulin sensitivity, increased inflammatory markers, slowed blood circulation
- **Above evidence is from observational studies, not RCTs; causal relationship not established**

## Do
1. Stand up and move for 5 minutes every 60 minutes (walk, stretch, squat)
2. Set alarms or use apps as reminders
3. Stand or walk while taking phone calls
4. Use a sit-stand desk, alternating positions (switch every 30 minutes)
5. Ensure at least 60 minutes of moderate-intensity activity daily (brisk walking, cycling, etc.)

## Don't
1. Don't assume "I exercise after work" offsets 8 hours of sitting—unless you reach 60–75 min/day of moderate-to-vigorous activity
2. Don't treat "standing desk" as the solution—standing all day offers no additional cardiovascular benefit and may harm lower limbs
3. Don't overthink "exercise intensity"—just start moving; that's what matters most

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent lower limb swelling after prolonged sitting (rule out deep vein thrombosis)
- Lumbar pain with lower limb numbness
- Dizziness upon standing after prolonged sitting (rule out orthostatic hypotension)
