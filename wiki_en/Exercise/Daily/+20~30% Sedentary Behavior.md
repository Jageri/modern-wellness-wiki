# Sedentary Behavior

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +20–30% (sitting >8 hours/day without exercise)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistent across numerous prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Get up and move for 5 minutes every 60 minutes of sitting. The harms of prolonged sitting cannot be fully offset by exercising after work.

## Evidence
- Lancet meta-analysis: Sitting >8 hours/day with no physical activity carries mortality risk comparable to smoking and obesity
- Those sitting >6 hours/day have ~20% higher mortality than those sitting <3 hours/day
- Key finding: The harm of prolonged sitting is independent of exercise volume—even 1 hour of daily exercise doesn't fully offset risk if the rest of the day is spent sitting
- Primary mechanisms: Reduced insulin sensitivity, increased inflammatory markers, slowed blood circulation

## Do
1. Stand up and move for 5 minutes every 60 minutes (walk, stretch, squat)
2. Set alarms or use apps as reminders
3. Stand or walk while taking phone calls
4. Use a sit-stand desk, alternating positions (switch every 30 minutes)

## Don't
1. Don't assume "I exercise after work" can offset 8 hours of daytime sitting—it can't fully compensate
2. Don't treat "standing desk" as the solution—standing all day offers no additional cardiovascular benefit and may harm lower limbs
3. Don't overthink "exercise intensity"—just start moving; that's what matters most

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent lower limb swelling after prolonged sitting (rule out deep vein thrombosis)
- Lumbar pain with lower limb numbness
- Dizziness upon standing after prolonged sitting (rule out orthostatic hypotension)
