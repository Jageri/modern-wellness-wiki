# Stretching / Flexibility

**Mortality Impact**: No direct mortality evidence, but reduces fall and injury risk
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐ (Limited evidence)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Stretch for 5 minutes before and after exercise. Poor flexibility is associated with increased fall risk. Dynamic stretching reduces exercise-related injury risk.

## Evidence
- Limited evidence linking poor flexibility to increased all-cause mortality
- Poor flexibility increases fall and injury risk (especially in older adults) [1]
- 2016 systematic review: Dynamic warm-ups reduce injury risk and improve joint range of motion [2]
- Static stretching after exercise improves recovery; pre-exercise static stretching does not clearly prevent injury

## Do
1. Dynamic stretching before exercise (leg swings, walking lunges)
2. Static stretching after exercise (30 seconds per movement)
3. Stretch neck, shoulders, and back after prolonged sitting

## Don't
1. Don't bounce-stretch (ballistic stretching)—easy to pull a muscle
2. Don't push through pain—stretch until you "feel it," not beyond
3. Don't use stretching as a substitute for aerobic or strength training

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent limited range of motion after stretching
- Increased pain after stretching
