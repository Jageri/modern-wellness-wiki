# Standing Desk

**Mortality Impact**: No additional cardiovascular benefit; may reduce some sedentary-related risk
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Inconsistent study conclusions)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Alternate between sitting and standing (switch every 30 minutes). Don't stand all day.

## Evidence
- Standing burns ~0.15 kcal/min more than sitting (very small difference)
- Prolonged standing may increase risk of lower limb varicose veins
- Optimal approach: sit-stand alternation + regular movement
- Standing alone cannot substitute for exercise

## Do
1. Alternate sitting and standing, switching every 30 minutes
2. Use an anti-fatigue mat when standing
3. Do light movements while standing (calf raises, weight shifting)

## Don't
1. Don't stand all day—no additional cardiovascular benefit, harms lower limbs
2. Don't assume standing at a desk counts as "exercise"

## Medical Red Flags
- Worsening varicose veins after prolonged standing
- Increased lower back pain after prolonged standing
