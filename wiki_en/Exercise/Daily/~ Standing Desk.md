# Standing Desk

**Mortality Impact**: No additional cardiovascular benefit; may reduce some sedentary-related risk
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Inconsistent study conclusions)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Alternate between sitting and standing (switch every 30 minutes). Don't stand all day.

## Evidence
- Standing burns ~0.15 kcal/min more than sitting (very small difference) [1]
- Replacing sitting with standing slightly reduces fasting blood glucose (-2.53 mg/dL) and body fat (-0.75 kg), but has no significant effect on blood pressure or lipids [2]
- Prolonged standing increases lower limb varicose vein risk (RR 1.78) [4] and orthostatic circulatory disease risk [3]
- Standing is not associated with cardiovascular disease risk (coronary heart disease, stroke, heart failure) — neither increases nor decreases [3]
- Optimal approach: sit-stand alternation + regular movement [1][3]
- Standing alone cannot substitute for exercise [2]
- **Note**: All studies above are observational or meta-analyses of clinical trials; causal relationships not established. Study populations are primarily European middle-aged and older adults; applicability to younger populations is uncertain

## Do
1. Alternate sitting and standing, switching every 30 minutes
2. Use an anti-fatigue mat when standing
3. Do light movements while standing (calf raises, weight shifting)

## Don't
1. Don't stand all day—no additional cardiovascular benefit, harms lower limbs
2. Don't assume standing at a desk counts as "exercise"

## Medical Red Flags
- Worsening varicose veins after prolonged standing
- Increased lower back pain after prolonged standing
