# Housework

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality approximately -20–30% for older men who do housework
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Observational studies)
**Actionability**: Very High

## Conclusion
Housework is one of the most underrated forms of "exercise." For older adults, it provides daily low-intensity physical activity and cognitive stimulation—both associated with longevity.

## Evidence
- Norwegian study of adults aged 65+ (~1,000 participants, 10-year follow-up): Older adults who did housework daily had significantly lower mortality than those who didn't
- Mechanism analysis: Housework combines physical activity (scrubbing, sweeping, carrying) + cognitive planning (organizing, arranging) + sense of purpose (maintaining household order)
- Gender difference: Men benefit more from housework—possibly because men are more likely to lose daily activity levels after retirement
- Blue Zones: People in longevity hotspots don't "exercise" but continuously engage in housework, gardening, and other daily physical activities
- Consistent with the "natural movement" philosophy: integrating exercise into daily life rather than going to the gym specifically

## Do
1. Don't outsource all housework—doing it yourself is free exercise
2. Older adults in particular should maintain housework habits
3. Gardening (a shared Blue Zones habit) has similar effects
4. No need to push to exhaustion—the cumulative effect of daily light activity is sufficient
5. Cooking counts too: meal preparation involves standing, walking, and fine motor hand movements
