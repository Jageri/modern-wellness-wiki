# Physical Inactivity

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +20–30%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistently recommended by WHO, AHA, etc.)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
150 minutes of moderate-intensity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week. Simply starting to move matters more than anything else.

## Evidence
- WHO: Insufficiently active individuals have a 20–30% increased risk of death compared to those who are sufficiently active [1]
- Lee 2012 Lancet global analysis: Physical inactivity causes ~9% of premature mortality worldwide (~5.3 million deaths per year) [2]
- Lancet Public Health 2022 meta-analysis (15 cohort studies, 47,471 adults): 6,000–8,000 steps/day (age ≥60) or 8,000–10,000 steps/day (age <60) were associated with significantly reduced all-cause mortality [3]
- Any exercise is better than none—the dose-response relationship is non-linear, with the greatest benefit going from zero to some
- **Note**: Above evidence is from observational studies; occupational physical activity may not confer the same protective effect ("physical activity paradox")

## Do
1. Start moving first; intensity is secondary
2. Strength training 2–3 times per week (targeting major muscle groups)
3. Combined aerobic + strength training is superior to either alone
4. Find a form of exercise you can stick with

## Don't
1. Don't overthink "exercise intensity" and fail to start
2. Don't do only aerobic and skip strength—muscle loss accelerates aging
3. Don't go high-intensity every day—recovery is equally important

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent fatigue lasting >48 hours after exercise
- Heart rhythm irregularities or persistent dizziness after exercise
- Persistent knee pain
