# Physical Inactivity

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +20–30%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistently recommended by WHO, AHA, etc.)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
150 minutes of moderate-intensity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week. Simply starting to move matters more than anything else.

## Evidence
- Lancet meta-analysis: Physically inactive individuals have 2× the mortality rate of active individuals
- 30 minutes of brisk walking daily can reduce all-cause mortality by ~20%
- Racket sports show the most significant mortality reduction (~47%)
- Any exercise is better than none—even 6,000 steps per day provides significant benefit

## Do
1. Start moving first; intensity is secondary
2. Strength training 2–3 times per week (targeting major muscle groups)
3. Combined aerobic + strength training is superior to either alone
4. Find a form of exercise you can stick with

## Don't
1. Don't overthink "exercise intensity" and fail to start
2. Don't do only aerobic and skip strength—muscle loss accelerates aging
3. Don't go high-intensity every day—recovery is equally important

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent fatigue lasting >48 hours after exercise
- Heart rhythm irregularities or persistent dizziness after exercise
- Persistent knee pain
