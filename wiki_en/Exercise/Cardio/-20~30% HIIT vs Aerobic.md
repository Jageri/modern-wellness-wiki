# HIIT vs Aerobic Exercise

**Mortality Impact**: Both significantly reduce all-cause mortality; HIIT is more time-efficient
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Multiple RCTs)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Both are beneficial. Those with an exercise base can add HIIT; beginners should start with brisk walking/jogging.

## Evidence
- Both reduce all-cause mortality by ~20–30%
- HIIT may be slightly superior to moderate-intensity aerobic exercise for improving cardiorespiratory fitness
- However, HIIT carries higher injury risk and greater recovery demands
- Optimal approach: combined aerobic + strength training

## Do
1. Aerobic: 150 minutes of moderate intensity per week (brisk walking, jogging, swimming, cycling)
2. HIIT: 1–2 sessions per week, 15–20 minutes each (only after building an exercise base)
3. Strength training: 2–3 times per week
4. Total volume matters more than intensity—just start moving

## Don't
1. Beginners should not jump straight into HIIT—high injury risk
2. Don't do HIIT every day—insufficient recovery leads to overtraining
3. Don't agonize over which is "best"—the exercise you can sustain is the best exercise

## Medical Red Flags
- Heart rhythm irregularities or persistent dizziness after exercise
- Dark urine after exercise (rhabdomyolysis)
- Persistent fatigue lasting >48 hours after exercise
