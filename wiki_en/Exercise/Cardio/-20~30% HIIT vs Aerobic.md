# HIIT vs Aerobic Exercise

**Mortality Impact**: Exercise (moderate or vigorous) vs inactivity reduces all-cause mortality by ~20-31%; direct HIIT vs MICT mortality comparison evidence is limited
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Strong evidence for exercise vs inactivity; preliminary evidence for HIIT vs MICT comparison)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Both are beneficial. Total exercise volume matters more than intensity. Those with an exercise base can add HIIT; beginners should start with brisk walking/jogging.

## Evidence
- Exercise (moderate-vigorous) vs inactivity: all-cause mortality reduced by ~20-31% (661,137 participants, dose-response relationship) [1]
- HIIT vs MICT direct comparison (RCT, ages 70-77): HIIT group mortality HR 0.63 (-37%, but CI 0.33-1.20, not significant); MICT group no benefit (HR 1.02) [2]
- HIIT superior to MICT for improving cardiorespiratory fitness (VO2peak) (29 RCT meta-analysis) [3]
- However, HIIT carries higher injury risk, greater recovery demands, and higher dropout rate (33% vs 26%) [2]
- Optimal approach: combined aerobic + strength training [1]

## Do
1. Aerobic: 150 minutes of moderate intensity per week (brisk walking, jogging, swimming, cycling)
2. HIIT: 1–2 sessions per week, 15–20 minutes each (only after building an exercise base)
3. Strength training: 2–3 times per week
4. Total volume matters more than intensity—just start moving

## Don't
1. Beginners should not jump straight into HIIT—high injury risk
2. Don't do HIIT every day—insufficient recovery leads to overtraining
3. Don't agonize over which is "best"—the exercise you can sustain is the best exercise

## Medical Red Flags
- Heart rhythm irregularities or persistent dizziness after exercise
- Dark urine after exercise (rhabdomyolysis)
- Persistent fatigue lasting >48 hours after exercise

- [3] IJERPH 2023. Effect of High-Intensity Interval Training vs. Moderate-Intensity Continuous Training on Fat Loss and Cardiorespiratory Fitness in the Young and Middle-Aged. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10048683/

## Note
Evidence is a mix of observational studies and RCTs. The exercise vs inactivity evidence comes from large observational cohorts (strong association but not RCT). The direct HIIT vs MICT mortality comparison comes from a single RCT (Generation 100) with non-significant results. Study populations are primarily European and American older adults; applicability to Asian younger populations is uncertain.
