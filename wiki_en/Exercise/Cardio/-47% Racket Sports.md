# Racket Sports

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality approximately -47% (highest among all exercise types)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Large prospective cohort study)
**Actionability**: Medium (requires courts and opponents)

## Conclusion
Among all exercise types, racket sports (badminton, table tennis, tennis) show the strongest association with reduced all-cause mortality, significantly outperforming running, swimming, and cycling.

## Evidence
- UK population cohort study (80,306 participants, Scotland + England): Racket sports associated with -47% all-cause mortality (HR 0.53, 95% CI 0.40-0.69) and -56% cardiovascular mortality (HR 0.44, 95% CI 0.24-0.83) [1]
- Comparison: Swimming -28% (HR 0.72), aerobics -27% (HR 0.73), cycling -15% (HR 0.85) (running did not show a significant association) [1]
- Badminton is especially worth promoting in China: easily accessible courts, strong social component, suitable for all ages
- Mechanism: Intermittent high intensity + hand-eye coordination (cognitive stimulation) + social interaction (psychological protection)
- **Note**: This is an observational study; causal relationship not established. Study population is British middle-aged adults (52±14 years, 54% female); applicability to other populations is uncertain

## Do
1. 2–3 times per week, 45–60 minutes per session
2. Badminton, table tennis, or tennis—choose what you enjoy
3. Doubles is better than singles (social benefit)
4. Warm up properly and protect your knees to avoid sports injuries
5. When no opponent is available, squash or wall practice also works

## References
