# Eggs

**Mortality Impact**: No significant impact in healthy populations
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Large prospective cohort studies and meta-analyses)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
1–2 whole eggs daily is safe for healthy populations. Don't discard the yolk.

## Evidence
- BMJ 2020 meta-analysis: 1 egg per day is not significantly associated with cardiovascular risk (Drouin-Chartier et al., BMJ 2020, PMID: 32132002)
- China Kadoorie Biobank study: Daily egg consumption associated with 11% lower CVD risk and 26% lower hemorrhagic stroke risk in Chinese adults (Qin et al., Heart 2018, PMID: 29785957)
- Eggs are an excellent source of high-quality protein, choline, and vitamin D
- Dietary cholesterol has limited effect on blood cholesterol for most people
- **Contradicting evidence**: A 2022 dose-response meta-analysis found 1 egg/day associated with 8% higher all-cause mortality and 16% higher cancer mortality, though evidence certainty was rated very low by GRADE (Yang et al., Nutr Rev 2022, PMID: 35178575)

## Do
1. 1–2 whole eggs daily
2. Boiled or steamed eggs are preferable to fried (less oil)
3. The yolk is the nutritional essence—don't discard it

## Don't
1. Don't discard the yolk—most nutrients are in the yolk
2. Don't eat raw—Salmonella risk and lower protein absorption
3. Those with diagnosed hypercholesterolemia or familial hyperlipidemia should limit intake as advised by their doctor

## Medical Red Flags
- Severe digestive discomfort after eggs (rule out allergy)
- Those with existing high cholesterol need regular follow-up testing
