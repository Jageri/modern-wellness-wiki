# Fruit & Vegetable Intake

**Mortality Impact**: 5 servings of fruits and vegetables daily can reduce all-cause mortality by ~15–26%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistent across numerous prospective studies and meta-analyses)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
At least 5 servings of fruits and vegetables per day (~400g), roughly half fruit and half vegetables. This is a common feature of virtually all longevity dietary patterns worldwide.

## Evidence
- Global Dietary Database (analysis of 540,000 deaths): 200g fresh fruit daily → mortality -17%
- Nature sub-journal (~60,000 participants, 23-year follow-up): Flavonoid-rich food intake associated with -20% mortality
- BMJ meta-analysis: 5 servings of fruits and vegetables daily → all-cause mortality -15%, cardiovascular mortality -20%
- Optimal range: 5 servings/day (~400g); marginal benefit diminishes beyond 5 servings
- Key nutrients: Dietary fiber, potassium, folate, vitamin C, polyphenols, carotenoids
- Vegetables ≠ potatoes (starchy vegetables have weaker protective effects)

## Do
1. At least 5 servings daily: 2 fruit + 3 vegetables (1 serving ≈ 80g)
2. The more colorful, the better: dark leafy greens, red tomatoes, orange carrots, purple eggplant
3. Prioritize local, seasonal produce (higher nutrient density)
4. Juice ≠ fruit (juice removes fiber and concentrates sugar)
5. Daily cup of tea + 1 apple + 100g blueberries + 100g broccoli = 500mg+ flavonoids
