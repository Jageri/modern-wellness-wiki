# White Meat

**Mortality Impact**: Replacing red meat with white meat can reduce all-cause mortality by 8–11%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Multiple large prospective studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Fish and poultry are healthier protein sources than red meat. This doesn't mean red meat is absolutely off-limits, but replacing red meat with white meat has clear mortality benefits.

## Evidence
- JAMA Internal Medicine (>500,000 participants): Increasing red meat by half a serving daily over 8 years → +10% all-cause mortality risk in the following 8 years; replacing half a serving of red meat with equivalent white meat → mortality -8–11%
- Additional fish benefits: Rich in EPA/DHA; cardiovascular protection is independent of protein itself
- Blue Zones data: Centenarians eat meat only ~5 times per month (mainly pork); most protein comes from legumes
- WHO classification: Processed meat is Group 1 carcinogen (same as tobacco); red meat is Group 2A
- Key distinction: White meatsuperior tored meat, but cooking method is equallyimportant—roasted chickensuperior tofried chicken

## Do
1. Limit red meat to ≤2–3 times per week; replace the rest with fish/poultry/legumes
2. Prioritize deep-sea fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines)—high EPA/DHA content
3. Remove poultry skin to reduce saturated fat
4. Cooking methods: steaming, boiling, roasting > frying, deep-frying
5. At least 2 fish meals per week
