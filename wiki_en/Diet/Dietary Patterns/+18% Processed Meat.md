# Processed Meat

**Mortality Impact**: Each 50g daily portion of processed meat → +18% colorectal cancer risk (RR=1.18, 95% CI 1.10–1.28)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Causal relationship established for colorectal cancer)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Processed meat (sausages, bacon, ham, etc.) is an IARC Group 1 carcinogen—best avoided.
> ⚠️ IARC Group 1 means there is sufficient evidence the agent causes cancer. It does NOT mean processed meat is as dangerous as tobacco or asbestos. Tobacco causes ~1 million cancer deaths/year globally; processed meat ~34,000. The classification describes strength of evidence, not magnitude of risk.

## Evidence
- IARC Monographs Volume 114 (2015): Processed meat classified as Group 1 carcinogen (carcinogenic to humans)—based on sufficient epidemiological evidence
- Each 50g/day of processed meat → +18% colorectal cancer risk (RR=1.18; 95% CI 1.10–1.28) [1]
- Primary carcinogens: Nitrites → N-nitroso compounds; high-temperature cooking produces polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) and heterocyclic amines (HCAs)
- Significantly associated with cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes risk (observational evidence; causal strength lower than for colorectal cancer)

## Do
1. Replace with fresh meat, poultry, or fish
2. Occasional consumption is acceptable, but don't make it a daily protein source
3. Check ingredient lists—be cautious of products containing "nitrites"

## Don't
1. Don't assume "a little is fine"—dose-response shows risk increases with intake; current data cannot determine whether a safe intake threshold exists
2. Don't treat "nitrate-free" labels as a guarantee—natural curing also contains nitrites
3. Don't feed children processed meat long-term

## Medical Red Flags
- Bloody stool, unexplained anemia
- Sudden change in bowel habits
- Colonoscopy screening recommended for those 45+
