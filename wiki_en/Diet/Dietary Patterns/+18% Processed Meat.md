# Processed Meat

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +18%; WHO Group 1 carcinogen
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Causal relationship established)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Don't eat it. There is no safe intake level for processed meats like sausages, bacon, and ham.

## Evidence
- WHO/IARC 2015: Processed meat classified as a Group 1 carcinogen (same category as tobacco)
- Each 50g of daily processed meat → +18% colorectal cancer risk
- Primary carcinogens: Nitrites, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, heterocyclic amines
- Significantly associated with cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes risk

## Do
1. Replace with fresh meat, poultry, or fish
2. Occasional consumption is acceptable, but don't make it a daily protein source
3. Check ingredient lists—be cautious of products containing "nitrites"

## Don't
1. Don't assume "a little is fine"—there is no safe amount
2. Don't treat "nitrate-free" labels as a guarantee—natural curing also contains nitrites
3. Don't feed children processed meat long-term

## Medical Red Flags
- Bloody stool, unexplained anemia
- Sudden change in bowel habits
- Colonoscopy screening recommended for those 45+
