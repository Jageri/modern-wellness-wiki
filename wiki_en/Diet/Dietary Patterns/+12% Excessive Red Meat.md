# Excessive Red Meat

**Mortality Impact**: Each 50g daily increase in red meat → all-cause mortality +12%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Prospective cohort studies; weaker evidence than processed meat)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
No more than 500g red meat per week (cooked weight). Prioritize poultry and fish.

## Evidence
- BMJ meta-analysis: Each 50g daily increase in red meat → all-cause mortality +12%
- Red meat is associated with colorectal cancer, cardiovascular disease, and type 2 diabetes risk
- Mechanisms: Iron overload, TMAO (gut metabolite), carcinogens produced by high-temperature cooking
- White meat (poultry, fish) does not show similar risk
- WHO classification: Processed meat is a Group 1 carcinogen; red meat is Group 2A

## Do
1. Partially replace red meat with poultry, fish, and legumes
2. Limit red meat to 300–500g per week (cooked weight)
3. Low-temperature cooking is preferable to high-temperature frying

## Don't
1. Don't eliminate red meat entirely—it's a good source of iron and B12
2. Don't eat processed meat (sausages, bacon are more dangerous than red meat)
3. Don't use high-temperature frying—produces more carcinogens

## References
- Zheng Y et al. Association of changes in red meat consumption with total and cause specific mortality. BMJ. 2019.
