# Glucosamine

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality approximately -15% (large observational study, healthy user bias present)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Large observational study; no RCT)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Glucosamine was originally used for joint health, and data suggests an association with lower all-cause mortality, though the effect size is limited and may be inflated by bias. Not recommended solely for longevity purposes.

## Evidence
- UK Biobank study (495,077 participants, median follow-up 8.9 years): Regular glucosamine users had all-cause mortality HR 0.85 (-15%), CVD mortality HR 0.82 (-18%), cancer mortality HR 0.94 (-6%), respiratory mortality HR 0.73 (-27%)
- Possible mechanisms: Inhibits NF-κB inflammatory pathway, lowers CRP, modulates gut microbiota
- **Key limitation**: Observational study; "healthy user bias" likely (people who take supplements tend to be healthier overall); no RCT validation
- Safety: Long-term use appears safe with minimal side effects

## Do
1. If you have joint issues, glucosamine is a reasonable supplement choice
2. Dosage: 1500mg/day (standard dose)
3. Choose sulfate form (better evidence than hydrochloride form)
4. Effect evaluation: Joint symptom improvement typically takes 2-3 months
5. Not recommended solely for longevity—await more RCT data

## References
