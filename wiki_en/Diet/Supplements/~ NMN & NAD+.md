# NMN / NAD+

**Mortality Impact**: No human mortality data; animal studies show lifespan extension effects
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐ (Strong animal study data; virtually zero human data)
**Actionability**: High (many supplements available on the market)

## Conclusion
NMN is a "star molecule" in the anti-aging field with impressive animal study results, but currently **there is no human evidence** proving it extends lifespan. It's expensive and efficacy is questionable—falls into the "maybe useful, but you might be wasting money" category.

## Evidence
- Mouse studies: NMN supplementation improves mitochondrial function, insulin sensitivity, and vascular function in aged mice; suppresses age-associated weight gain; improves eye function and bone density (Mills 2016, 12-month administration)
- Human clinical trials (current results):
  - NMN can increase blood NAD+ levels (confirms effective absorption)
  - Yoshino 2021 RCT (n=25, 10 weeks) reported NMN improved muscle insulin sensitivity in postmenopausal women with prediabetes, but the trial was criticized: hepatic lipid content differed significantly between NMN and placebo groups (6.3% vs 14.8%, P=0.003), indicating flawed randomization (Brenner 2021)
  - **2024 systematic review and meta-analysis** (8 RCTs, 342 adults, 250–2000mg/d, 14 days to 12 weeks): NMN showed **no significant improvement** in fasting glucose, fasting insulin, HbA1c, HOMA-IR, or lipid profile (Chen 2024)
  - Results for muscle function and exercise performance are **inconsistent**
  - **No human trial has shown NMN reduces mortality or extends lifespan**
- Core issue: Mouse lifespan experiments only require 2–3 years; human validation requires decades
- Safety: Short-term trials (<1 year) have found no serious side effects, but long-term safety is unknown. **Note:** Patients with active tumors should exercise caution—NAD+ may provide energy to tumor cells and assist DNA repair
- Regulatory: FDA previously excluded NMN from dietary supplements (as it was being investigated as a drug), then reversed the decision; regulatory status remains unclear
- Cost: 200–800 RMB per month; cost-effectiveness is questionable

## Do
1. **Not recommended for the general public**—await long-term human data
2. If you must try: Choose reputable brands, dosage 250–500mg/day
3. Prioritize investing in interventions with strong existing evidence: exercise, diet, sleep
4. Follow progress on David Sinclair lab's human trials
5. Note: NMN ≠ NR (nicotinamide riboside); they have similar but not identical mechanisms
