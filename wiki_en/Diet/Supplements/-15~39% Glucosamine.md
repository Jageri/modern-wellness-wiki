# Glucosamine

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality approximately -15–39% (observational studies; healthy user bias exists)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Large observational studies; lacking RCTs)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Glucosamine was originally used for joint health; data suggests it is associated with reduced all-cause mortality, but the effect size may be overestimated. Not recommended solely for the purpose of longevity.

## Evidence
- UK Biobank study (466,039 participants, 8-year follow-up): Regular glucosamine users had all-cause mortality HR 0.61 (-39%), cardiovascular mortality -58%, cancer mortality -19%
- NHANES cohort study: Glucosamine supplement users had -18% all-cause mortality
- Possible mechanisms: Inhibits NF-κB inflammatory pathway, lowers CRP levels, improves gut microbiota
- **Important limitation**: The -39% comes from a single observational study and may reflect "healthy user bias" (people who take supplements are generally healthier overall); the independent NHANES study showed only -18%; no RCT validation exists
- Safety: Good long-term safety profile with minimal side effects

## Do
1. If you have osteoarticular issues, glucosamine is a reasonable supplement choice
2. Dosage: 1500mg/day (standard dose)
3. Choose the sulfate form (more evidence than the hydrochloride form)
4. Effect evaluation: Joint symptom improvement typically takes 2–3 months
5. Not recommended solely for longevity purposes—await more RCT data

## References
