# Vitamin D Deficiency

**Mortality Impact**: No significant impact on all-cause mortality (BMJ meta-analysis)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Large meta-analysis)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Don't expect it to extend lifespan. But it is indeed important for bone health and calcium absorption; those who are deficient should supplement.

## Evidence
- BMJ meta-analysis: Vitamin D supplementation has no significant effect on all-cause mortality
- However, vitamin D deficiency is clearly associated with osteoporosis and increased fracture risk
- Vitamin D deficiency rate in Chinese population is ~60–80% (especially in northern regions during winter)
- Vitamin D supplementation can improve calcium absorption and reduce fracture risk (in specific populations)

## Do
1. Get 15–20 minutes of gentle sun exposure daily
2. Those who are deficient (25-hydroxyvitamin D <20 ng/mL) should supplement 800–1000 IU/day
3. Dietary sources: Deep-sea fish, egg yolks, fortified milk

## Don't
1. Don't expect vitamin D to extend lifespan—evidence doesn't support it
2. Don't overdose (>4000 IU/day)—may cause hypercalcemia
3. Don't treat vitamin D as a cure-all supplement

## Medical Red Flags
- Bone pain, recurrent fractures
- 25-hydroxyvitamin D <20 ng/mL
- Persistent deficiency despite supplementation (rule out malabsorption)
