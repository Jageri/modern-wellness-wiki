# High Salt / High Sodium

**Mortality Impact**: Significantly increased cardiovascular mortality risk
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (INTERSTROKE and other large studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
<5g salt per day (approximately one bottle cap). Watch for hidden salt: soy sauce, pickled foods, processed foods.

## Evidence
- INTERSTROKE study: High-sodium diet is the primary modifiable risk factor for stroke
- Each 1g/day increase in sodium intake increases cardiovascular risk by ~6%
- Average salt intake among Chinese residents is ~10.5g/day, 2× the WHO recommendation
- Salt reduction can lower systolic blood pressure by 5–10 mmHg

## Do
1. Use a salt measuring spoon for quantification
2. Replace some salt with vinegar, lemon, and spices
3. Eat fewer pickled and processed foods
4. Check sodium content on nutrition labels

## Don't
1. Don't assume it's fine if you don't eat pickles—processed foods are the biggest source of hidden salt
2. Don't use "low-sodium salt" and eat freely—excess potassium also has risks
3. Don't overlook the salt content in condiments like soy sauce and oyster sauce

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent hypertension (>140/90 mmHg)
- Recurrent edema
- Poorly controlled hypertension despite medication
