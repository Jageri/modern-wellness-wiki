# Betel Nut

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality approximately +21% (betel nut chewers)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Meta-analysis, strong causal relationship)
**Actionability**: High (quittable)

## Conclusion
Betel nut is a Group 1 carcinogen (same category as tobacco), clearly increasing oral cancer risk and all-cause mortality. There is no safe dose—even "occasional chewing" carries risk.

## Evidence
- PLoS ONE meta-analysis: Betel nut chewers have +21% all-cause mortality
- WHO/IARC classified betel nut as a Group 1 carcinogen (2003)
- Oral cancer risk: Betel nut chewers have 2–10× increased oral cancer risk (depending on concurrent smoking/alcohol use)
- Chinese data: Provinces with high betel nut consumption (e.g., Hunan) have significantly higher oral cancer incidence than the national average
- Mechanism: Arecoline → oral mucosal fibrosis → precancerous lesions → oral cancer
- Betel nut + tobacco + alcohol: Synergistic effect, oral cancer risk multiplied

## Do
1. Do not start chewing betel nut
2. For current chewers: Quit as soon as possible; the earlier, the greater the benefit
3. Withdrawal symptoms (if any) typically resolve within 1–2 weeks
4. Regular oral checkups: Long-term chewers should be screened for oral mucosal lesions
5. Note: The "stimulating" effect of betel nut products is not worth the cancer risk
