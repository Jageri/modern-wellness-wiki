# Excessive Alcohol Consumption

**Mortality Impact**: No safe level of alcohol consumption; clear causal relationship
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (The Lancet Global Burden of Disease study)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Zero alcohol is optimal. If unavoidable, limit to ≤100g pure alcohol per week (~5 beers), and never drink on an empty stomach.

## Evidence
- The Lancet 2018: Analysis of 2400+ data sources; zero alcohol minimizes health risk
- Stanford 2026: No safe level of alcohol consumption; every single drink is harmful
- Leading causes of death: Cirrhosis, esophageal cancer, breast cancer, cardiovascular disease
- Acetaldehyde (alcohol metabolism intermediate) is a Group 1 carcinogen

## Do
1. If you can avoid it, don't drink
2. If you must drink: Eat first, control the pace, pair each drink with a glass of water
3. Stick to one type of alcohol; don't mix
4. Have at least 2 completely alcohol-free days per week (give the liver recovery time)

## Don't
1. Don't believe "a little red wine is good for the heart"—this has been debunked
2. Don't take hangover cures and drink freely—no hangover product has been clinically validated
3. Don't drink on an empty stomach—alcohol absorption rate doubles

## Medical Red Flags
- Liver area pain, jaundice after drinking
- Memory decline after long-term drinking
- Withdrawal symptoms (hand tremor, sweating, anxiety)
