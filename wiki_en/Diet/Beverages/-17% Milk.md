# Milk

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality approximately -10–17% (moderate dairy consumers)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Large prospective cohort studies, but with conflicts of interest)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
1–2 servings of dairy per day (milk, yogurt, or cheese) is associated with reduced cardiovascular risk. Those with lactose intolerance can choose yogurt or cheese.

## Evidence
- PURE study (21 countries, 130,000 participants): 2 servings of dairy per day → all-cause mortality -17%, cardiovascular mortality -23%, stroke risk -33%
  - **Conflict of interest**: PURE study was partially funded by food industry organizations including the Canadian Dairy Association; conclusions need independent verification
- Swedish cohort study: >3 cups of milk/day vs <1 cup: no significant difference in female mortality, slight increase in males (U-shaped curve)
- Key distinction: Fermented dairy (yogurt, cheese) may have stronger protective effects than plain milk
- Mechanisms: Calcium, vitamin D, K2, probiotics (fermented products), conjugated linoleic acid
- Controversy: Whole fat vs skim—current evidence tends toward whole fat not being harmful

## Do
1. 1–2 servings of dairy per day (1 serving = 244g milk/yogurt, 15g cheese, 5g butter)
2. Prioritize fermented dairy (yogurt, kefir, cheese)
3. For lactose intolerance: Choose yogurt, hard cheese, or lactase supplements
4. Don't drink large amounts of milk on an empty stomach (may worsen lactose intolerance symptoms)

## References
- Dehghan M et al. Association of dairy intake with cardiovascular disease and mortality in 21 countries (PURE). Lancet. 2018.
- Michaelsson K et al. Milk intake and risk of mortality and fractures in women and men. BMJ. 2014.
