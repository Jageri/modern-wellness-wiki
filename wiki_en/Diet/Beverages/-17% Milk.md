# Milk

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality approximately -10–17% (moderate dairy consumers)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Large prospective cohort studies, but with conflicts of interest)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
1–2 servings of dairy per day (milk, yogurt, or cheese) is associated with reduced cardiovascular risk. Those with lactose intolerance can choose yogurt or cheese. Fermented dairy (yogurt, cheese) has stronger health benefits than plain milk.

## Evidence
- PURE study (21 countries, 136,000 participants): >2 servings/day vs no intake → all-cause mortality -17% (HR 0.83, 95%CI 0.72-0.96), cardiovascular mortality -23% (HR 0.77, 0.58-1.01), stroke risk -34% (HR 0.66, 0.53-0.82)
  - **Conflict of interest**: PURE study was partially funded by food industry organizations including the Canadian Dairy Association; conclusions need independent verification
- Swedish cohort study (Michaëlsson 2014): 61,433 women + 45,339 men. Women ≥3 cups/day vs <1 cup → all-cause mortality HR 1.93 (1.80-2.06); per glass of milk: women HR 1.15 (1.13-1.17), men HR 1.03 (1.01-1.04). **High milk intake was associated with increased fracture risk in women** (per glass hip fracture HR 1.09, 1.05-1.13)
- High-fat milk meta-analysis (Naghshi 2022): High-fat milk associated with increased all-cause mortality (ES 1.15, 1.09-1.20), CVD mortality (ES 1.09, 1.02-1.16), and cancer mortality (ES 1.17, 1.08-1.28); however total dairy intake reduced CVD mortality (ES 0.93, 0.88-0.98)
- Total dairy and mortality meta-analysis (Cavero-Redondo 2019): Total dairy consumption not significantly associated with all-cause mortality (RR 0.96-1.01 per 200g/day)
- Latest dose-response meta-analysis (Frontiers Nutrition 2026, 29 cohorts, 1.68M participants): Yogurt 200g/day reduces all-cause mortality 11% (HR 0.89) and CVD mortality 11% (HR 0.89); cheese 15g/day reduces CVD mortality 5% (HR 0.95); total dairy shows U-shaped association, optimal intake ~250-300g/day
- Key distinction: Fermented dairy (yogurt, cheese) has stronger protective effects than plain milk, especially high-fat milk
- Mechanisms: Calcium, vitamin D, K2, probiotics (fermented products), conjugated linoleic acid
- Controversy: Whole fat vs skim—high-fat milk may increase mortality risk, but fermented dairy (cheese, yogurt) does not show this harm

## Do
1. 1–2 servings of dairy per day (1 serving = 244g milk/yogurt, 15g cheese, 5g butter)
2. Prioritize fermented dairy (yogurt, kefir, cheese)
3. For lactose intolerance: Choose yogurt, hard cheese, or lactase supplements
4. Don't drink large amounts of milk on an empty stomach (may worsen lactose intolerance symptoms)
5. Avoid high consumption of high-fat milk (>3 cups/day); choose low-fat or fermented alternatives
