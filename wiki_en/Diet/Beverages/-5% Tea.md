# Tea

**Mortality Impact**: May reduce all-cause mortality by ~5–10%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Primarily from observational studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Both green and black tea are beneficial; light tea is preferable. Tea polyphenols have antioxidant effects, but don't expect tea to "extend your life."

## Evidence
- Large Chinese population cohort: Daily tea drinkers have ~5–10% lower all-cause mortality
- EGCG in green tea has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties
- Tea may be associated with reduced cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes risk
- However, observational studies have many confounders (tea drinkers tend to have healthier lifestyles overall)

## Do
1. Light tea is preferable; drink 1 hour after meals
2. Both green and black tea are beneficial
3. Can serve as a replacement for sugary drinks

## Don't
1. Don't drink strong tea—affects iron absorption
2. Don't take medications with tea—may affect drug efficacy
3. Don't drink strong tea on an empty stomach—irritates the stomach

## Medical Red Flags
- Severe insomnia, heart palpitations after tea
- Iron deficiency anemia symptoms after long-term tea consumption
