# Tea

**Mortality Impact**: May reduce all-cause mortality by ~5–15%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Large prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Both green and black tea are beneficial; light tea is preferable. Tea polyphenols have antioxidant effects, but don't expect tea to "extend your life."

## Evidence
- Shanghai Men's/Women's Health Study (Zhao et al., 2017): Green tea inversely associated with all-cause mortality (HR 0.95, 95%CI 0.90–1.01; never-smokers HR 0.89, 0.82–0.96), CVD mortality HR 0.86 (0.77–0.97) — PMID: 28135196
- China-PAR Project (Wang et al., 2020): 100,902 Chinese adults, habitual tea drinkers had all-cause mortality HR 0.85 (0.79–0.90), ASCVD incidence HR 0.80 (0.75–0.87), 1.41 more ASCVD-free years — PMID: 31914807
- UK Biobank (Inoue-Choi et al., 2022): 498,043 participants, ≥2 cups/day associated with ~13% lower all-cause mortality (HR 0.87, 0.84–0.91) — PMID: 36037472
- Green tea EGCG has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties (Mokra et al., 2022 review) — PMID: 36613784
- Tea ≥4 cups/day may reduce type 2 diabetes risk (Li et al., 2023 meta-analysis; overall RR 0.96 [0.91–1.00], significant in ≥4 cups/day subgroup) — PMID: 37647847
- However, all are observational studies; confounders cannot be fully excluded (tea drinkers tend to have healthier lifestyles)

## Do
1. Light tea is preferable; drink 1 hour after meals
2. Both green and black tea are beneficial
3. Can serve as a replacement for sugary drinks

## Don't
1. Don't drink strong tea—affects non-heme iron absorption (drinking 1 hour after meals mitigates this)
2. Don't take medications with tea—green tea catechins inhibit OATP transporters and enhance P-gp efflux, reducing absorption of many drugs (e.g., atorvastatin, digoxin, folic acid)
3. Don't drink strong tea on an empty stomach—irritates the stomach
4. Anemic patients or those taking iron supplements should avoid tea with meals

## Medical Red Flags
- Severe insomnia, heart palpitations after tea
- Iron deficiency anemia symptoms after long-term tea consumption
- Unusual drug efficacy changes when consuming large amounts of tea while on prescription medications
