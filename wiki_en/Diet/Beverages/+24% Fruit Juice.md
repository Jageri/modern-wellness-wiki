# Fruit Juice

**Mortality Impact**: Each additional daily serving of fruit juice increases all-cause mortality by +24% (more dangerous than sugary drinks)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Multiple large prospective studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
100% pure fruit juice is not a "health food." Once the fiber is removed, juice is sugar water—fructose absorption is even faster than from sugary drinks.

## Evidence
- JAMA Network Open (REGARDS cohort, n=13,440): Each additional 12 oz of daily fruit juice → all-cause mortality +24% (HR 1.24, 95%CI 1.09-1.42); sugary drinks were +11% (HR 1.11, 95%CI 1.03-1.19)
- Why fruit juice shows stronger mortality association than sugary drinks: higher natural fructose concentration, no fiber buffer
- BMJ (NutriNet-Santé cohort, n=101,257): Sugary drinks associated with +18% overall cancer risk (HR 1.18, 95%CI 1.10-1.27); 100% fruit juice also increased cancer risk by +12% (HR 1.12, 95%CI 1.03-1.23)
- Fructose metabolism specifics: Primarily metabolized in the liver; excess → fatty liver → insulin resistance → metabolic syndrome
- Distinction: Whole fruit ≠ juice (whole fruit has fiber; chewing slows absorption)
- ⚠️ Counter-evidence: D'Elia 2021 meta-analysis (21 prospective studies + 35 RCTs) found 100% fruit juice NOT associated with higher CV risk; moderate consumption (≤200ml/day) may even reduce stroke risk and lower blood pressure

## Do
1. Eating whole fruit is better than drinking juice
2. If occasionally drinking juice: Limit to <100ml; don't drink on an empty stomach
3. Vegetable juice is better than fruit juice (lower sugar, more fiber retained)
4. Giving children juice ≠ healthy—AAP (American Academy of Pediatrics) recommends no juice for children under 1 year
5. Commercial "100% fruit juice" doesn't mean healthy—sugar content may be higher than cola
