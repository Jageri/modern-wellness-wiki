# Coffee

**Mortality Impact**: Moderate consumption may reduce all-cause mortality by ~10–15%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistent across numerous prospective studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
1–3 cups of coffee per day is safe for most people and may have liver-protective effects. Don't drink after 3 PM.

## Evidence
- Multiple large cohort studies: 2–3 cups of coffee per day associated with 10–15% lower all-cause mortality
- Coffee reduces risk of cirrhosis, liver cancer, type 2 diabetes, and Parkinson's disease
- Liver protection supported by meta-analysis (each additional cup per day reduces liver cancer risk by ~15%)
- Pregnant women, those with severe anxiety disorders, and those with heart rhythm irregularities should limit intake

## Do
1. 1–3 cups of black or lightly sweetened coffee daily
2. Finish by 3 PM (half-life is ~5 hours)
3. Choose paper-filtered coffee (reduces cafestol, more cholesterol-friendly)

## Don't
1. Don't add lots of sugar and cream—calories negate health benefits
2. Don't drink after 3 PM—affects sleep
3. Don't use coffee as a substitute for sleep

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent heart palpitations or severe insomnia after coffee
- Worsened acid reflux after coffee
