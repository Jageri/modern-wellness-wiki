# Sugary Drinks

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +10–20%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Multiple prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Don't drink them. Liquid sugar is the fastest-absorbing and most easily over-consumed form of sugar.

## Evidence
- JAMA Internal Medicine: 1 sugary drink per day → all-cause mortality +7%
- 2+ drinks per day: all-cause mortality +21%
- Liquid sugar doesn't trigger satiety signals, making overconsumption easy
- Significantly associated with obesity, type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and gout

## Do
1. Replace with water, unsweetened tea, or black coffee
2. If you want something sweet, eat fruit (fiber slows sugar absorption)
3. Check ingredient lists—sucrose, high-fructose corn syrup, and concentrated fruit juice are all sugar

## Don't
1. Don't drink "diet drinks" unlimited—the long-term effects of artificial sweeteners remain controversial
2. Don't assume fresh-squeezed juice is healthy—it concentrates sugar and removes fiber
3. Don't overlook "hidden sugar": flavored yogurt, flavored milk, sports drinks

## Medical Red Flags
- Recurrent thirst, frequent urination (rule out diabetes)
- Elevated uric acid, gout attacks
