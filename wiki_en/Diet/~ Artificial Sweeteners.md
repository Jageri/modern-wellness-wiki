# Artificial Sweeteners

**Mortality Impact**: Conflicting evidence; some studies suggest potential harm
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐ (High research heterogeneity)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Occasional use is fine, but don't treat them as "harmless sugar" for long-term heavy consumption.

## Evidence
- WHO 2023: Does not recommend artificial sweeteners for weight control (long-term efficacy unconfirmed) [1]
- Some studies: Long-term heavy consumption of artificial sweeteners associated with increased cardiovascular risk (BMJ 2022, NutriNet-Santé cohort) [2]
- Artificial sweeteners may alter gut microbiota and maintain preference for sweet taste
- However, short-term use (e.g., as a sugar substitute for diabetic patients) may be a reasonable choice
- **Note**: The above are observational studies; causal relationships have not been established

## Do
1. Use occasionally; don't make them the primary component of daily beverages
2. Gradually reduce dependence on sweet taste
3. Water and unsweetened tea are the best beverages

## Don't
1. Don't assume "0 sugar = healthy"—artificial sweeteners are not harmless
2. Don't give children artificial sweeteners long-term—affects taste development
3. Don't consume artificial sweetener beverages unlimited

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent bloating, diarrhea after artificial sweetener use
- Weight gain despite using artificial sweeteners
