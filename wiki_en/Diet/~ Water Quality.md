# Water Quality

**Mortality Impact**: Significant regional variation; minimal impact in developed areas
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐ (Significant regional variation)
**Actionability**: Medium

## Conclusion
Boil before drinking; clean water dispensers regularly. Don't believe in "alkaline water" or "hydrogen-rich water."

## Evidence
- Water contamination (heavy metals, bacteria, chemicals) remains an important health risk in less developed areas
- Tap water in developed areas is generally safe after treatment
- Concepts like alkaline water, hydrogen-rich water, and micro-clustered water lack high-quality clinical evidence
- Water dispensers that aren't cleaned regularly may harbor bacteria

## Do
1. Boil tap water before drinking
2. Clean water dispensers regularly (every 1–2 months)
3. Use certified water purifiers in areas with poor water quality

## Don't
1. Don't believe in "alkaline water" or "hydrogen-rich water"—it's all marketing
2. Don't drink untreated groundwater long-term
3. Don't stockpile large quantities of bottled water (microplastic + environmental concerns)

## Medical Red Flags
- Recurrent gastrointestinal discomfort after drinking water
- Pay attention to official announcements after local water source contamination events
