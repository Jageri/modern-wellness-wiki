# Chili Peppers

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality approximately -23% (eating spicy food >4 times per week)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistent across multiple large cohort studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Regular spicy food consumption is associated with significantly lower all-cause and cardiovascular mortality. Capsaicin is the core active component.

## Evidence
- US NHANES cohort (16,179 participants, 19-year follow-up): Those eating spicy >4 times/week vs non-spicy eaters had -23% all-cause mortality and -34% cardiovascular mortality
- Italian Moli-sani study (22,811 participants): Eating spicy >4 times/week associated with -23% all-cause mortality and -34% ischemic heart disease mortality
- China Kadoorie Biobank study (487,375 participants): Eating spicy 5–7 days/week vs <1 day/week, all-cause mortality -14%
- Core mechanism: Capsaicin activates TRPV1 receptors → promotes fat oxidation, reduces inflammation, improves insulin sensitivity
- Note: These are observational studies; spicy food consumers may have overall healthier diets

## Do
1. Eat spicy food 3–5 times per week
2. Fresh peppers, dried peppers, chili powder, chili sauce all work
3. Capsaicin is heat-stable; cooking doesn't affect its activity
4. Those with sensitive stomachs should start gradually; avoid large amounts on an empty stomach
5. Note: High-oil, high-salt spicy foods (e.g., hotpot base) may have their health benefits offset
