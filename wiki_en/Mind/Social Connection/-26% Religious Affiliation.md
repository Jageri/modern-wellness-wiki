# Religious Affiliation

**Mortality Impact**: Regular religious service attendees have 4–14 years longer life expectancy and -20–30% all-cause mortality
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Multiple large prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: Varies by individual

## Conclusion
The protective effect of religious affiliation doesn't come from "what you believe," but from "belonging"—the combined effect of community, ritual, purpose, and social support. Those without religious faith can obtain similar benefits through other community activities.

## Evidence
- US Nurses' Health Study (74,534 women, 20-year follow-up): Attending religious services ≥1 time/week vs never → all-cause mortality -26%, life expectancy +5 years
- Blue Zones: 4 of 5 longevity regions have strong faith communities; 100% of centenarians belong to some faith group
- JAMA Internal Medicine: Attending religious services ≥1 time/week → cardiovascular mortality -20%, cancer mortality -10%
- Mechanisms: Regular social contact + sense of purpose + stress-relief rituals + lifestyle regularization + social pressure to quit smoking/drinking
- Atheist alternatives: Volunteer activities, community clubs, meditation groups—the core is "regular, belonging-based social connection"

## Do
1. For believers: Attend religious services regularly (≥1 time/week)
2. For non-believers: Join aregularcommunity—book clubs, sports clubs, volunteer organizations
3. What matters isn't "what you believe," but "regular social connection with a sense of belonging"
4. When lonely: Having a pet is also an effective alternative (pet companionship reduces cardiovascular risk)
