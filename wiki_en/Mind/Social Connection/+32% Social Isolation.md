# Social Isolation

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +32% (Nature Human Behaviour meta-analysis)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (90 studies, 2 million+ adults)
**Actionability**: Medium

## Conclusion
Maintain at least one deep social relationship. The damage of social isolation to lifespan is comparable to smoking (the exact magnitude is debated, but the scale is similar).

## Evidence
- Nature Human Behaviour 2023 meta-analysis: Social isolation → +32% premature death risk; loneliness → +14%
- JAMA Network Open 2024: Social isolation is a stronger predictor of premature death than loneliness, depression, or anxiety
- WHO 2025: One-sixth of the global population is experiencing loneliness; loneliness causes ~100 deaths per hour
- Strong social connections → 50% higher survival rate than isolated individuals

## Do
1. At least 1 in-person social interaction per week (not online)
2. Maintain relationships where you can have deep conversations; quality > quantity
3. Join interest-based communities or volunteer activities
4. Those living alone: maintain regular contact with family and friends

## Don't
1. Don't treat "I don't need people" as independence—may be avoidance
2. Don't substitute social media "likes" for genuine connection
3. Don't ignore signals of "chronic loneliness"

## Medical Red Flags
- Long-term isolation + worsening mood + self-neglect
- Social avoidance lasting more than 1 month
- Loneliness accompanied by persistent insomnia or appetite changes
