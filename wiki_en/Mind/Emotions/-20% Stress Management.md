# Stress Management

**Mortality Impact**: Chronic stress increases cardiovascular disease and all-cause mortality through inflammatory pathways (independent risk factor)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Extensive mechanistic research and epidemiological data)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Stress isn't the problem—chronic, unrelievable stress is. Blue Zones centenarians all have daily stress-relief rituals—what matters is "conscious release," not "no stress."

## Evidence
- Chronic stress → sustained cortisol elevation → chronic inflammation → comprehensive increase in cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disease risk
- Whitehall II study: High work stress → +67% cardiovascular event risk
- Mindfulness meditation meta-analysis: Lowers cortisol, improves immune function, reduces blood pressure
- Blue Zones stress-relief methods: Okinawans honor ancestors daily, Seventh-day Adventists pray, Ikarians nap, Sardinians enjoy afternoon wine
- Key finding: Stress itself doesn't kill; the lack of stress-relief mechanisms does

## Do
1. Establish a daily stress-relief ritual (pick one and stick with it):
   - Mindfulness meditation: 10–20 minutes daily (Apps: Headspace, Calm)
   - Breathing exercises: 4-7-8 technique (inhale 4 seconds, hold 7 seconds, exhale 8 seconds)
   - Journaling: Write 3 things you're grateful for daily
   - Walking: 20 minutes in a natural environment daily
2. Social connection is one of the best stress relievers (chat with friends, family dinners)
3. Don't use alcohol/medication to de-stress—this backfires
4. Learn to say "no"—reduce unnecessary stressors
