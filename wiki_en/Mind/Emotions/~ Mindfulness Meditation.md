# Mindfulness Meditation

**Mortality Impact**: May reduce cardiovascular risk; limited direct mortality evidence
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (RCTs support blood pressure and stress reduction)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
5–10 minutes of mindfulness meditation daily can reduce stress and blood pressure. Don't pursue "a blank mind."

## Evidence
- AHA scientific statement: Meditation may reduce cardiovascular risk (Evidence Level B)
- RCTs show mindfulness meditation can reduce systolic blood pressure by ~4–5 mmHg
- Lowers cortisol levels, improves anxiety and depression symptoms
- However, insufficient evidence for direct effects on all-cause mortality

## Do
1. Start with 5–10 minutes daily
2. Just focus on your breath; when your mind wanders, bring it back
3. Use apps for guidance (Headspace, Calm, etc.)

## Don't
1. Don't pursue "a completely blank mind"—mind-wandering is normal
2. Don't force yourself to meditate whenextremelyanxious—may increase anxiety
3. Don't use meditation as a substitute for proper psychological treatment

## Medical Red Flags
- Increased anxiety or dissociation after meditation
- Meditation triggering traumatic memory flashbacks
