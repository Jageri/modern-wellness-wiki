# Depression / Chronic Stress

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +29% to +110% (depression), depending on study design and confounder control
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Multiple prospective studies, but publication bias and confounding controversy exist)
**Actionability**: Medium

## Conclusion
Persistent low mood + loss of interest for >2 weeks: seek medical help proactively. Daily management uses exercise + social connection + mindfulness.

## Evidence
- Large-scale meta-analyses show elevated all-cause mortality in depression: Cuijpers 2014 (293 studies) adjusted RR=1.52 (95% CI: 1.45-1.59); Chan 2025 (268 studies) unmatched RR=2.10 (95% CI: 1.87-2.35), matched for comorbidities RR=1.29 (95% CI: 1.21-1.37)
- Chronic stress elevates cortisol via HPA axis → damages immune, cardiovascular, and metabolic systems
- Depression is significantly associated with cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and cancer risk (observational evidence)
- Social isolation and depression show a bidirectional association, mutually reinforcing as risk factors

### ⚠️ Limitations & Controversies
- **Counter-evidence**: Miloyan & Fried 2017 reanalyzed 3.6 million participants and found that after controlling for comorbid mental disorders and health behaviors, the depression-mortality association disappeared. However, this is a secondary analysis, not an independent cohort
- **Publication bias**: Cuijpers 2014 found publication bias inflated the effect by ~8% (unadjusted RR=1.64 → adjusted RR=1.52)
- **Confounding**: Observational studies cannot rule out reverse causation (illness causing depression rather than depression causing death) or residual confounding
- **Population heterogeneity**: Psychotic depression (RR=1.61) and treatment-resistant depression (RR=1.27) carry higher risk; effects are smaller in older adults (RR≈1.34)

## Do
1. Maintain social connections; at least 1 deep conversation per week
2. Regular exercise: the 2024 BMJ network meta-analysis (Noetel et al.) confirmed walking/jogging (g=-0.62), yoga (g=-0.55), and strength training (g=-0.49) have moderate effects on depression and should be considered alongside psychotherapy and antidepressants as core treatments
3. 10 minutes of mindfulness meditation daily (MBCT has evidence for preventing depression relapse)
4. Maintain regular schedule; ensure adequate sleep

## Don't
1. Don't tell yourself to "just cheer up"—it's not an attitude problem, it's an illness
2. Don't use alcohol to "suppress" anxiety—it will worsen
3. Don't stop medication on your own—adjustments must be made under medical supervision

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent low mood + loss of interest for >2 weeks
- Panic attacks
- Thoughts of self-harm
