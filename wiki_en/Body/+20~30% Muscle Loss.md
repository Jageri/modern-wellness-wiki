# Muscle Loss / Sarcopenia

**Mortality Impact**: Sarcopenia patients have significantly elevated mortality after falls and fractures
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Geriatric medicine consensus)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
1.0–1.2g/kg protein per meal + strength training 2–3 times per week. Muscle is the best "insurance."

## Evidence
- 3–8% muscle mass loss per decade after age 40
- Sarcopenia patients have 2–3× increased fall risk
- ~20% mortality within 1 year of hip fracture
- Grip strength is a strong predictor of all-cause mortality

## Do
1. Sufficient protein intake at each meal (evenly distributed across three meals)
2. Strength training 2–3 times per week (squats, deadlifts, and other compound movements)
3. Higher protein needs for those 65+ (1.2–1.5 g/kg)
4. Vitamin D supplementation (supports muscle function)

## Don't
1. Don't assume "getting thinner is just part of aging"—muscle loss ≠ normal aging
2. Don't do only aerobic exercise without strength training—aerobic exercise cannot prevent muscle loss
3. Don't fear "bulking up"—normal training won't cause that

## Medical Red Flags
- Noticeable grip strength decline (can't open bottle caps)
- Difficulty standing up,significantly slower walking speed
- Unexplained weight loss
