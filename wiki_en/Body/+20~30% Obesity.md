# Obesity (BMI >30)

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +20–30%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Nurses' Health Study and other large cohorts)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Caloric deficit (300–500 kcal/day) + strength training to preserve muscle. Don't crash diet; focus on body fat percentage rather than weight.

## Evidence
- BMI >30 associated with ~20–30% increased all-cause mortality
- Waist circumference is a better predictor of cardiovascular risk than BMI (visceral fat is key)
- Dieting rebound rate >80%, and each rebound lowers metabolic rate
- Strength training + moderate caloric deficit is the most sustainable fat loss approach

## Do
1. Daily caloric deficit of 300–500 kcal (no more)
2. Protein intake 1.2–1.6 g/kg body weight (preserve muscle)
3. Strength training 2–3 times per week
4. Focus on waist circumference, not weight

## Don't
1. Don't crash diet (<1200 kcal/day)—you'll rebound harder
2. Don't focus only on weight—muscle is heavier than fat
3. Do not blindly trust "diet pills"—most lack long-term evidence

## Medical Red Flags
- Normal BMI but extreme fear of weight gain (rule out body dysmorphic disorder)
- Rapid unexplained weight gain (rule out endocrine issues)
- Eating disorder symptoms after weight loss
