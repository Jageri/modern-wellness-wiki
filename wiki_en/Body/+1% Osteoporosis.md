# Osteoporosis

**Mortality Impact**: ~20% mortality within 1 year of hip fracture
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Osteoporosis clinical consensus)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Calcium supplementation (food first) + vitamin D + weight-bearing exercise. All three are essential.

## Evidence
- Globally, 1 osteoporotic fracture occurs every 3 seconds
- ~20% mortality within 1 year of hip fracture (primary causes: complications, immobility-related infections)
- ~1/3 of women over 50 will experience an osteoporotic fracture
- Peak bone mass is reached around age 30, after which it continuously declines

## Do
1. Daily calcium intake 1000–1200mg (milk, tofu, dark leafy greens)
2. Vitamin D: 15–20 minutes of sun exposure daily; supplement 800–1000 IU/day if deficient
3. Weight-bearing exercise (walking, running, strength training—bones need mechanical stimulation)
4. Regular bone density testing after age 50

## Don't
1. Don't just take calcium supplements without exercise—bones need mechanical stimulation to retain calcium
2. Don't assume "calcium = bone soup"—bone soup has extremely low calcium content
3. Don't neglect vitamin D—without it, calcium absorption drops by 75%

## Medical Red Flags
- Height loss >3cm, worsening kyphosis
- Fracture from minor trauma
- Bone density T-score <-2.5
