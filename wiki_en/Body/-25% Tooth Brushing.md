# Tooth Brushing

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality approximately -20–25% (regular tooth brushers)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistent across multiple prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: Very High

## Conclusion
Oral health is strongly correlated with systemic diseases. Regular tooth brushing is one of the simplest and cheapest life-extension measures—cost is nearly zero, but the benefits are enormous.

## Evidence
- UK cohort study (>10,000 participants, 14-year follow-up): Brushing ≥2 times/day vs irregular brushers → all-cause mortality -25%
- Periodontal disease and cardiovascular disease: Periodontal disease patients have 20–30% increased cardiovascular risk (independent of other risk factors)
- Periodontal disease and diabetes: Bidirectional relationship; poorly controlled periodontal disease makes blood glucose harder to manage
- Periodontal disease and Alzheimer's: Porphyromonas gingivalis (P. gingivalis) has been found in the brains of Alzheimer's patients
- Mechanism: Chronic oral inflammation → elevated systemic inflammatory markers → vascular endothelial damage → atherosclerosis

## Do
1. Brush twice daily, 2 minutes each time
2. Use fluoride toothpaste
3. Use dental floss or interdental brushes daily (interdental cleaning is more important than brushing itself)
4. Professional dental cleaning 1–2 times per year
5. Bleeding gums are a sign of periodontal disease—don't stop brushing because of bleeding; brush more
6. Electric toothbrush is superior to manual (supported by evidence)
