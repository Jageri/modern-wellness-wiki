# Oral Health

**Mortality Impact**: Periodontal disease associated with cardiovascular disease and diabetes risk; tooth loss linked to increased all-cause mortality (adjusted HR 1.02–1.30)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Clear association; causal relationship pending RCT confirmation)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Brush twice daily + floss once daily + professional cleaning every 6 months. The mouth is a window to overall health, though RCT evidence that periodontal treatment directly reduces cardiovascular events is currently insufficient.

## Evidence

### Periodontal Disease → Cardiovascular Disease
- Fewer remaining teeth → higher risk of atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ACVD) and all-cause mortality (ACM): pooled RR for ACVD 1.69–2.93, ACM 1.76–2.27; multivariable-adjusted HR for ACVD 1.02–1.21, ACM 1.02–1.30 [PMID 34026863]

### Periodontal Disease ↔ Diabetes (Bidirectional)
- WHO-funded Lancet systematic review (28 longitudinal studies, >300,000 participants):
  - Periodontitis → **19–26%** higher incidence of type 2 diabetes
  - Diabetes → tooth loss HR 1.11–1.16
  - Edentulism → type 2 diabetes RR 1.30 [PMID 41865743]

### Oral Bacteria → Systemic Inflammation
- Bacteremia incidence: dental extractions 62–66%, scaling/root planing 36–44%, toothbrushing 8–26%, flossing 16%; peak within 5 minutes post-procedure [PMID 36750413]

### Toothbrushing Frequency → CVD Outcomes
- In CAD patients, brushing once vs ≥twice daily → 3P-MACE HR 1.71 (95% CI 1.18–2.46) [PMID 40425644]
- Toothbrushing frequency inversely associated with CVD risk and inflammatory markers (CRP, fibrinogen) (Scottish Health Survey, BMJ 2010)

### Tooth Loss → Nutrition & Function
- Tooth loss impairs chewing function and nutritional intake, indirectly elevating all-cause mortality risk [PMID 22086361]

## ⚠️ Important Caveats (Counter-Evidence)
- Non-surgical periodontal therapy (NSPT) reduces inflammatory biomarkers (CRP, IL-6), but **4 of 5 studies evaluating clinical ASCVD hard endpoints did NOT show superiority of NSPT** over less intensive care [PMID 41953585]
- Observational studies cannot fully exclude residual confounding (poor oral hygiene correlates with lower SES, higher smoking rates)
- Association ≠ causation: whether periodontal treatment prevents CVD events requires large, long-follow-up RCTs

## Do
1. Brush twice daily (Bass technique)
2. Floss once daily
3. Professional dental cleaning every 6 months
4. Regular dental checkups

## Don't
1. Don't use a hard-bristle toothbrush with horizontal scrubbing—damages gums
2. Don't ignore bleeding gums—may be a sign of periodontal disease
3. Don't assume "losing teeth is normal with age"—it's preventable

## Medical Red Flags
- Recurrent gum bleeding
- Loose teeth
- Oral ulcers not healing after >2 weeks
