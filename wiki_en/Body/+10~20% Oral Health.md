# Oral Health

**Mortality Impact**: Periodontal disease associated with cardiovascular disease and diabetes risk
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Clear association; causal relationship to be confirmed)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Brush twice daily + floss once daily + professional cleaning every 6 months. The mouth is a window to overall health.

## Evidence
- Periodontal disease associated with ~20% increased cardiovascular disease risk
- Periodontal disease and diabetes have a bidirectional relationship (each is a risk factor for the other)
- Oral bacteria can enter the bloodstream, triggering systemic inflammation
- Missing teeth lead to reduced nutritional intake and chewing function

## Do
1. Brush twice daily (Bass technique)
2. Floss once daily
3. Professional dental cleaning every 6 months
4. Regular dental checkups

## Don't
1. Don't use a hard-bristle toothbrush with horizontal scrubbing—damages gums
2. Don't ignore bleeding gums—may be a sign of periodontal disease
3. Don't assume "losing teeth is normal with age"—it's preventable

## Medical Red Flags
- Recurrent gum bleeding
- Loose teeth
- Oral ulcers not healing after >2 weeks
