# Vision Protection

**Mortality Impact**: No direct mortality evidence, but affects quality of life
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Strong evidence for myopia prevention)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds) + outdoor activity. Myopia isn't just about "not seeing clearly."

## Evidence
- High myopia (>600 degrees) significantly increases risk of retinal detachment, glaucoma, and cataracts
- Outdoor activity is the strongest protective factor against juvenile myopia
- The 20-20-20 rule can alleviate digital eye strain
- Global myopia rates continue to rise;By 2050, an estimated 50% of the population will be myopic

## Do
1. 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds
2. 2 hours of outdoor activity daily (especially important for adolescents)
3. Maintain 50–70 cm distance between screen and eyes
4. Regular eye exams

## Don't
1. Don't look at your phone in the dark
2. Don't ignore changes in myopia prescription
3. Do not excessively rely on blue-light-blocking glasses

## Medical Red Flags
- Sudden vision loss, worsening floaters, visual field defects
- Regular fundus exams for those with high myopia
