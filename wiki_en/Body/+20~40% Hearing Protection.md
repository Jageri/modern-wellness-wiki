# Hearing Protection

**Mortality Impact**: Moderate or greater hearing loss associated with ~20–40% increased all-cause mortality and 2–5× increased cognitive decline risk
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Large prospective studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Hearing loss isn't just about "not hearing clearly"—it comprehensively affects health through social isolation, increased cognitive load, and elevated fall risk. Timely use of hearing aids can reverse most of the risk.

## Evidence
- Lancet Dementia Commission: Hearing loss is the largest modifiable risk factor for dementia (attributable risk 8%)
- US ARIC study (moderate hearing loss): All-cause mortality increased by ~20%
- ACHIEVE trial (ages 70–84, 3-year RCT): Hearing aid intervention group had 48% slower cognitive decline
- Mechanism: Auditory deprivation → social withdrawal → reduced cognitive stimulation → accelerated brain atrophy
- Noise exposure: Prolonged headphone use above 85 dB causes permanent hearing damage
- Hearing aid usage rate is extremely low: Only ~20% of those who need them use them

## Do
1. Protect hearing: Headphone volumenot exceeding max volume  60%; continuous usenot exceeding 60 minutes
2. Use earplugs or noise-canceling headphones in noisy environments
3. Annual hearing check after age 50
4. For those with hearing loss: Use hearing aids as early as possible (the earlier the better; the brain needs time to readjust)
5. Don't think "I can still hear so it's fine"—hearing loss is gradual; by the time it's obvious, it's already advanced
