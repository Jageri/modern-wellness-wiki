# Thermoregulation (Cold/Heat Exposure)

**Mortality Impact**: Extreme temperature exposure increases mortality; moderate cold/heat exposure may be beneficial
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Strong evidence for extreme temperatures; limited evidence for moderate exposure)
**Actionability**: Medium

## Conclusion
Avoid extreme temperature exposure. Moderate cold/heat exposure may activate the body's adaptive mechanisms, but don't overdo it.

## Evidence
- Extreme heat waves: Cause tens of thousands of deaths annually (heat stroke, cardiovascular events)
- Extreme cold: Increases cardiovascular events and respiratory disease risk
- Sauna: Finnish studies show 4–7 sessions/week associated with ~50% lower cardiovascular mortality (observational studies)
- Cold exposure: May activate brown fat and boost metabolism (limited evidence)

## Do
1. Hot weather: Hydrate, replenish electrolytes, avoid midday sun
2. Cold weather: Stay warm, pay attention to cardiovascular protection
3. Sauna/cold baths: Start gradually

## Don't
1. Don't push through extreme temperatures
2. Don't suddenly attempt extreme cold exposure (cardiovascular risk)
3. Don't push through heat stroke (body temperature >40°C is a medical emergency)

## Medical Red Flags
- Heat stroke (body temperature >40°C, confusion)
- Persistent shivering, heart palpitations after cold exposure
- Chest pain, difficulty breathing after extreme temperature exposure
