# Cold Showers

**Mortality Impact**: No direct mortality data; may reduce sick days
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐ (Small-sample studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Try gradually. May activate brown fat and increase alertness, but don't expect it to "extend lifespan."

## Evidence
- PLOS ONE: 30-day cold shower challenge reduced sick days by 29%
- Cold exposure activates brown fat and increases norepinephrine secretion
- However, small sample sizes and lack of long-term follow-up
- Cardiovascular risk patients should use caution (cold stimulation may trigger arrhythmias)

## Do
1. Gradually lower temperature from warm water (start with 15 seconds)
2. Morning cold exposure is more effective
3. A shower is sufficient; no need for ice baths

## Don't
1. Don't suddenly attempt extreme cold exposure—cardiovascular risk
2. Don't try while sick
3. Don't assume cold showers can replace exercise

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent shivering, heart palpitations after cold exposure
- Chest pain after cold shower
