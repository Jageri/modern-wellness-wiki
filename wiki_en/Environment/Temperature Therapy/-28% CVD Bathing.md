# Bathing (Hot Bath)

**Cardiovascular Risk**: CVD incidence -28%, stroke incidence -26% (daily bathers vs ≤2 times/week)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: Very High

## Conclusion
The health mechanisms of hot baths overlap significantly with saunas—improving vascular function through heat exposure. When sauna facilities are unavailable, hot baths are an effective alternative.

## Evidence
- Japanese JPHC study (30,076 participants, 19-year follow-up, 1990-2009): Daily bathers vs ≤2 times/week → CVD incidence risk -28% (HR 0.72, 95% CI 0.62-0.84), stroke incidence risk -26% (HR 0.74, 95% CI 0.62-0.87)
- **Note**: The above data are for CVD **incidence** risk, not mortality. The primary outcome of this study was incident CVD. Direct effects of bathing on all-cause mortality lack independent studies
- Temperature: In this study, Japanese bathing water temperatures were typically 40-41.6°C (104-107°F), with no statistically significant effect of temperature on outcomes. However, extrapolating from heat exposure mechanisms, water temperatures above ~40°C may be necessary for cardiovascular benefit
- Mechanism: Similar to sauna—heat exposure → vasodilation → blood pressure reduction → cardiac workload mimics exercise
- Additional benefit: Improves sleep quality (core body temperature rises then falls after bathing, promoting sleep onset)
- 2019 meta-analysis: Hot bath 1-2 hours before bedtime (40-42.5°C) can shorten sleep onset time by ~10 minutes

## Caveats
- Study population was middle-aged Japanese (40-59 years); generalizability to other ethnicities and age groups requires further validation
- Individuals with unstable angina, uncontrolled hypertension, or serious heart conditions should avoid hot baths
- Those over 70 with low blood pressure (systolic ~110 mmHg or below) should exercise caution and avoid excessively hot water

## Do
1. Hot bath daily or every other day, 15-20 minutes each
2. Water temperature 40-42°C (no need to scald yourself)
3. Bathing 1-2 hours before bedtime is most effective (uses temperature drop to promote sleep)
4. Cardiovascular disease patients should start with warm water and gradually increase temperature
5. Rehydrate after bathing
