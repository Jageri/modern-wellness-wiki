# Dry Sauna

**Mortality Impact**: Cardiovascular mortality approximately -50% (4–7 sessions/week), all-cause mortality -40%
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Large prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: Medium (Requires sauna facilities)

## Conclusion
The cardiovascular protection from sauna is comparable to moderate-intensity exercise. Finnish research provides the strongest evidence—Finland has 3 million saunas for 5.3 million people.

## Evidence
- Finnish Kuopio Ischemic Heart Disease Risk Factor Study (2,315 men, 20-year follow-up): 4–7 sauna sessions/week vs 1 session → all-cause mortality -40%, cardiovascular mortality -50%
- Core mechanism: Heat exposure → heart rate rises to 100–150 bpm (similar to moderate exercise) → improved vascular endothelial function → blood pressure reduction → decreased inflammatory markers
- Peter Attia's recommendation: 4 times per week, 20 minutes each, above 82°C (179°F)
- Infrared sauna effects may be similar, but evidence is less robust than traditional dry sauna
- Note: Those with severe cardiovascular disease should consult their doctor

## Do
1. 3–4 times per week, 15–20 minutes each
2. Temperature above 80°C for optimal effect
3. Hydrate thoroughly before and after sauna
4. Never sauna after alcohol consumption (significantly increases sudden death risk)
5. If no sauna access, hot baths are an alternative (slightly weaker effect but supported by data)
