# Sun Exposure

**Mortality Impact**: Those who avoid sun exposure have ~2× the all-cause mortality of regular sun-exposed individuals (HR 2.0)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistent across large prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
10–30 minutes of unprotected sun exposure daily (depending on skin tone, latitude, and UV index); benefits outweigh risks. Excessive sun avoidance may be more harmful than moderate sun exposure.

## Evidence
- Swedish MISS cohort 20-year follow-up (29,518 women): Sun avoiders had ~2× all-cause mortality compared with the highest sun exposure group (HR ≈2.0), comparable to smoking [1]
- UK Biobank prospective study (419,007 participants): Higher habitual UV exposure associated with significantly lower all-cause mortality (HR 0.84, 95% CI 0.82–0.87), lower cardiovascular mortality, and no clear increase in skin cancer mortality [2]
- Mechanism 1 (vitamin D): UVB stimulates cutaneous synthesis of vitamin D₃ → hepatic conversion to 25(OH)D → renal activation to 1,25(OH)₂D. Low 25(OH)D levels associated with 50–100% increased all-cause mortality (multiple meta-analyses)
- Mechanism 2 (nitric oxide): UVA irradiation mobilizes nitric oxide (NO) stored in skin into the circulation, causing vasodilation, lowering blood pressure, and improving vascular function—independent of vitamin D [3]
- Danish 26-year follow-up study: Even patients who developed skin cancer from excessive sun exposure lived an average of ~6 years longer than the general population (note: observational data, causal relationship not fully established) [4]
- **Important caveat**: The above data are from observational studies; sun avoiders may have other unhealthy behaviors (residual confounding). However, the UK Biobank study substantially mitigated confounding through multivariable adjustment and counterfactual modeling
- Optimal time window: When local UV index > 3 (rough rule: your shadow is shorter than your height). In most regions this corresponds to 10 AM – 3 PM, when UVB proportion peaks and vitamin D synthesis is most efficient

## Risks (Counter-Evidence)
- UV radiation is the primary environmental risk factor for non-melanoma skin cancers (BCC, SCC). A Danish occupational cohort estimated 27% of BCC and 11% of SCC cases may be attributable to occupational solar UV exposure
- Melanoma is more strongly linked to intermittent intense sun exposure (especially sunburn history during adolescence) than to regular, moderate sun exposure
- Core principle: Avoid sunburn, not sun exposure

## Do
1. 10–30 minutes of unprotected sun exposure daily (darker-skinned individuals and those at high latitudes need more time)
2. UV index must be > 3 (shadow shorter than your height) for effective UVB-driven vitamin D synthesis
3. Avoid sunburn; apply sunscreen or cover up after the safe exposure window
4. Consider vitamin D₃ supplementation in winter or at high latitudes when UV index remains < 3 (1,000–4,000 IU/day)
5. Test 25(OH)D levels via blood test; target >30 ng/mL (>75 nmol/L)
6. Sun exposure through glass is ineffective—ordinary glass blocks UVB, preventing vitamin D synthesis
