# Sun Exposure

**Mortality Impact**: Those who avoid sun exposure have ~2× the all-cause mortality of regular sun-exposed individuals (HR 2.0)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Consistent across large prospective cohort studies)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
10–30 minutes of sun exposure daily (depending on skin tone and latitude); benefits outweigh risks. Excessive sun avoidance may be more harmful than moderate sun exposure.

## Evidence
- Swedish 20-year follow-up (29,518 women): Sun avoiders had all-cause mortality HR 2.0 (i.e., ~2× the death risk of regular sun-exposed individuals), comparable to smokers
- Mechanism: UVB synthesizes vitamin D + UVA releases nitric oxide (lowers blood pressure, improves vascular function)
- Vitamin D and all-cause mortality: Low levels associated with 50–100% increased mortality (multiple meta-analyses)
- Even patients who developed skin cancer from excessive sun exposure lived an average of 6 years longer than the general population (Danish 26-year follow-up study)
- **Note**: The above data are from observational studies; sun avoiders may have other unhealthy behaviors; causal relationship not fully established
- Optimal time window: 10 AM – 3 PM (primarily UVB, highest synthesis efficiency)

## Do
1. 10–30 minutes of unprotected sun exposure daily (darker-skinned individuals need more time)
2. Avoid sunburn; apply sunscreen after 30 minutes
3. Consider vitamin D supplementation in winter or high-latitude regions (1000–4000 IU/day)
4. Test 25(OH)D levels via blood test; target >30 ng/mL
5. Sun exposure through glass is ineffective (glass blocks UVB)
