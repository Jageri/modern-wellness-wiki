# Noise Pollution

**Mortality Impact**: Cardiovascular mortality risk +10% (long-term exposure)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Epidemiological studies)
**Actionability**: Low

## Conclusion
Use earplugs in long-term noisy environments; soundproof the bedroom. Noise isn't just annoying—it genuinely harms your health.

## Evidence
- Each 10 dB increase in traffic noise → ~10% increase in cardiovascular risk
- Long-term noise exposure raises cortisol, blood pressure, and inflammatory markers
- Nighttime noise is more damaging to sleep quality and cardiovascular health than daytime noise
- WHO estimates: Europe loses 1.6 million healthy life years annually due to traffic noise

## Do
1. Use noise-canceling earplugs in long-term noisy environments
2. Soundproof the bedroom (double-glazed windows, thick curtains)
3. Keep the bedroom quiet at night (<30 dB)

## Don't
1. Don't use loud headphones to "cover" noise—double hearing damage
2. Don't ignore long-term low-intensity noise—chronic harm

## Medical Red Flags
- Tinnitus, hearing loss
- Persistent headaches after noise exposure
