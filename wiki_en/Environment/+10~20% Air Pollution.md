# Air Pollution

**Mortality Impact**: All-cause mortality +10–20% (high PM2.5 exposure areas)
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Extensive epidemiological studies)
**Actionability**: Low (individuals can hardly change the broader environment, but can take protective measures)

## Conclusion
Wear an N95 mask when AQI >150; run an air purifier indoors. In the long term, living in areas with better air quality is more conducive to longevity.

## Evidence
- Global Burden of Disease study: Air pollution is the 4th leading global mortality risk factor
- Each 10 μg/m³ increase in PM2.5 → ~6% increase in all-cause mortality
- Leading causes of death: Cardiovascular disease, respiratory disease, lung cancer
- Winter smog in northern China is significantly associated with life expectancy differences

## Do
1. Monitor AQI; reduce outdoor activities when >150
2. Wear an N95 mask on heavily polluted days
3. Use an air purifier indoors (with HEPA filter)
4. If possible, choose to live in areas with better air quality

## Don't
1. Don't exercise outdoors on heavily polluted days—harm outweighs benefit
2. Don't ignore indoor air pollution (cooking fumes, formaldehyde from renovations)
3. Don't substitute regular masks for N95—insufficient PM2.5 filtration

## Medical Red Flags
- Persistent cough, chest tightness after smog
- Respiratory symptoms that don't resolve after air quality improves
