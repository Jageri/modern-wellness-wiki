# Socioeconomic Status

**Mortality Impact**: The poorest populations have 1.5–3× the all-cause mortality of the wealthiest
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (One of the strongest and most consistent health determinants globally)
**Actionability**: Low (difficult to change at the individual level, but awareness matters)

## Conclusion
Income and education level are among the strongest predictors of mortality—stronger than most dietary and exercise factors. This isn't about "money buys life," but rather the combined effect of chronic stress from poverty, lack of healthcare access, and adverse environments.

## Evidence
- GBD 2019: Low socioeconomic status is a top risk factor for global disease burden
- Whitehall study (British civil servants): Lowest-ranking vs highest-ranking → 2–3× mortality increase
- US data: Bottom 20% income vs top 20% → ~10–15 year life expectancy gap
- Education effect: Each additional year of education → ~2–3% reduction in mortality
- Mechanisms: Chronic stress + healthcare access + living environment + diet quality + health literacy + social support networks

## Do (What you can control at the individual level)
1. Health literacy: Understand basic health knowledge (you're reading this encyclopedia—you're already doing this)
2. Regular checkups: Early detection and treatment is key to bridging the healthcare access gap
3. Social network: Proactively build supportive social relationships (doesn't cost much)
4. Educate yourself: Continuous learning (in any form) is itself a health-protective factor
5. Don't give up on health management because "no money"—free exercise, sleep, and social connection are equally effective
