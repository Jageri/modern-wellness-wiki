# Microplastics

**Mortality Impact**: Long-term effects unclear; no definitive mortality data currently
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐ (Emerging research area)
**Actionability**: Medium

## Conclusion
Use fewer plastic containers to heat food; drink less bottled water. Long-term effects are still under investigation.

## Evidence
- Microplastics have been detected in human blood, placenta, and lung tissue
- Animal studies: Microplastics may trigger inflammation and oxidative stress
- However, large-scale population studies proving microplastics directly cause death are currently lacking
- Bottled water contains significantly higher microplastic levels than tap water

## Do
1. Use fewer plastic containers to heat food (especially in microwaves)
2. Prefer glass or stainless steel containers
3. Drink less bottled water; use filtered tap water

## Don't
1. Don't put hot water in plastic bottles
2. Don't microwave plastic food containers
3. Don't panic excessively—current evidence is insufficient to prove acute harm

## Medical Red Flags
- Currently no acute symptoms requiring medical attention (this is a chronic exposure issue)
