# Napping

**Mortality Impact**: Moderate napping may be beneficial; excessive napping may be harmful
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Inconsistent study conclusions)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
10–20 minutes is optimal; complete before 3 PM. Naps exceeding 1 hour may increase cardiovascular risk.

## Evidence
- Heart journal: Napping >60 minutes associated with ~30% increased cardiovascular risk
- However, 10–20 minute naps can improve alertness and work efficiency
- Excessive napping may be a signal of underlying conditions (e.g., sleep apnea)
- The association between Mediterranean nap culture and cardiovascular benefits is unclear

## Do
1. Limit to 10–20 minutes
2. Complete before 3 PM
3. Set an alarm to avoid oversleeping

## Don't
1. Do not nap exceeding 1 hour—affects nighttime sleep, may increase cardiovascular risk
2. Do not nap face-down—damages the cervical spine
3. Don't use naps as a substitute for nighttime sleep

## Medical Red Flags
- Feeling more tired or groggy after napping
- Unable to maintain daytime alertness without napping (rule out sleep disorders)
