# Blue Light Exposure

**Mortality Impact**: No direct evidence; primarily acts indirectly through sleep disruption
**Evidence Level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Clear indirect mechanisms)
**Actionability**: High

## Conclusion
Reduce screen use 2 hours before bed. Blue-light-blocking glasses have limited effectiveness; reducing screen time is the fundamental solution.

## Evidence
- Blue light suppresses melatonin secretion, delaying sleep onset
- Meta-analysis: Blue-light-blocking glasses have limited effectiveness in improving sleep quality
- The real problem is the stimulating nature of screen content, not just blue light itself
- Long-term poor sleep quality is associated with increased all-cause mortality

## Do
1. Reduce screen use 2 hours before bed
2. Enable night mode on phone/computer
3. No electronic devices in the bedroom

## Don't
1. Don't rely solely on blue-light-blocking glasses—limited effectiveness
2. Don't assume enabling night mode means you can scroll infinitely

## Medical Red Flags
- Severe insomnia despite improving blue light exposure
-Persistent vision decline
